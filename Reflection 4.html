REFLECTION 4:
Userinyerface - Worst UI Practice

To be honest, I don’t know how I feel about the quiz we had. I’m not sure if I loved it or I absolutely hated it. It was like a complete waste of time but also very informative to us USERDES students. The game sort of became a “Not-To-Do” list for designing interfaces and it really highlighted how important it is that users don’t have a hard time using or accessing our apps or pages.

The first part for me was pretty simple. Being a person who watches anime every second, minute, and hour, I’m used to reading a lot of text around the screen. For example, the anime’s subtitle, translations on screen, or other texts in the anime. Because of this, I have no problem reading EVERYTHING that I see on screen and then know which bits of information I should retain or just forget. I made sure I read all the instructions which made the first part pretty easy. I can fill out online registrations with my eyes closed because of all the online games I’ve registered for. The other sections were also easy, the key is to take note of every detail and then proceed. UI design got worse every section from slow scrolling in the terms and conditions, to selecting age through a scroll function. The worst part is the captcha part of the game. Even a robot with no emotion would get annoyed by having to check more than 10 pictures just to verify itself. I just used my common sense and eventually finished the test. I think I was the first one who finished; with age comes wisdom hahaha jk(I’m the oldest in my block). To sum everything up, it was a lot of fun and I hope we continue working on things like that.
