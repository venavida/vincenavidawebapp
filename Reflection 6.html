REFLECTION 6:
First Half of USERDES 


For me, USERDES is one of the most interesting classes I’ve had in APC. USERDES has taught me to look at different users’ perspectives when designing. When I use different apps and look at them, I see them in a deeper way now because of all the things I’ve learned from our class; I see flaws, and I also see the good things about their designs. Our class has shown me how important designing really is.

Some of the challenges I’ve encountered are working with groupmates that I mostly didn’t choose, and coding. I’ve never been good at leading people in a field that I’m not very good at, unlike music, where I can put my hands on everything and make things work somehow. I also did not go to Senior High School so I don’t know a lot about HTML and other things about coding. The last time I heard about those things were in 5th grade. I think the rest of my classmates have an edge when it comes to these things because they learned about them in SHS.

My challenges aren’t really making me feel sad or anything severe, I just feel incompetent sometimes when I can’t do what’s asked of me. I often question myself if I’m really cut out to be an IT student or not. For now, I’ll do my best and work on the things that I have to work on.

I expect that the finals will be rough on everyone which is why everyone needs to step up individually and as groups. I think that our finals will have a lot of coding, as well as brainstorming for designs. We also have to improve our reporting skills.

I’m really not sure what we need to improve on and work on as a class. I think we’re doing well as we are and we’re at a good pace that most of us can follow. Our activities are really helpful in honing our designing and writing skills. My classmates and I appreciate that Ms. Jen teaches us things and prepares activities unlike others who just play videos and expect us to learn like magic.
